Signal Words

Learn the key words that indicate when to use each tense

Past Simple

yesterday last week ago in 2020 then when

Example: "I went to school yesterday and studied last week."

Past Continuous

while when at 3 PM all morning during as

Example: "I was studying while you were sleeping all morning."

Past Perfect

before after already by the time until when

Example: "I had finished my homework before you called me."

Past Perfect Continuous

for since all day how long before when

Example: "I had been studying for hours before the exam."

Present Simple

always usually often sometimes never every day

Example: "I always go to school and never miss classes."

Present Continuous

now at the moment currently right now today this week

Example: "I am studying now and working at the moment."

Present Perfect

already just yet ever never since

Example: "I have already finished my homework and just arrived."

Present Perfect Continuous

for since all day lately recently how long

Example: "I have been studying for 3 hours and since morning."

Future Simple

tomorrow next week will shall soon in the future

Example: "I will go to school tomorrow and study next week."

Future Continuous

at 3 PM this time tomorrow will be + ing when while during

Example: "I will be studying at 3 PM this time tomorrow."

Future Perfect

by 5 PM by then by the time before until when

Example: "I will have finished by 5 PM and completed by then."

Future Perfect Continuous

for 8 hours by then will have been + ing since for how long

Example: "I will have been studying for 8 hours by then."

Comparison Tables

Compare similar tenses side-by-side to understand the differences

Simple vs Continuous

Tense Usage Signal Words Example
Present Simple Habits, facts, general truths always, usually, every day I go to school every day.
Present Continuous Actions happening now now, at the moment, currently I am going to school now.
Past Simple Completed actions in the past yesterday, last week, ago I went to school yesterday.
Past Continuous Actions in progress in the past while, when, at 3 PM I was going to school when you called.
Future Simple Predictions and decisions tomorrow, next week, will I will go to school tomorrow.
Future Continuous Actions in progress in the future at 3 PM, this time tomorrow I will be going to school at 3 PM.

Perfect vs Perfect Continuous

Tense Focus Duration Example
Present Perfect Completed action with present relevance Result matters I have gone to school.
Present Perfect Continuous Ongoing action with duration Duration matters I have been going to school for years.
Past Perfect Action completed before another past action Sequence matters I had gone to school before you called.
Past Perfect Continuous Action ongoing before another past action Duration before past I had been going to school for years.
Future Perfect Action completed before future time Completion by future I will have gone to school by 5 PM.
Future Perfect Continuous Action ongoing up to future time Duration until future I will have been going to school for 8 hours.

Time Reference Comparison

Time Simple Continuous Perfect Perfect Continuous
Past I went to school. I was going to school. I had gone to school. I had been going to school.
Present I go to school. I am going to school. I have gone to school. I have been going to school.
Future I will go to school. I will be going to school. I will have gone to school. I will have been going to school.

Common Confusion Points

Confusion Correct Usage Incorrect Usage Why?
Present Perfect vs Past Simple I have lived here for 5 years. I lived here for 5 years. Present perfect shows connection to now
Will vs Going to I will go to school tomorrow. I am going to go to school tomorrow. Will for predictions, going to for plans
Past Simple vs Past Perfect I had finished before you arrived. I finished before you arrived. Past perfect shows sequence
Present Simple vs Present Continuous I work as a teacher. I am working as a teacher. Simple for permanent, continuous for temporary
